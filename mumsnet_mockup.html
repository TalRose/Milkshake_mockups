(13 Posts)
Add message | Report
Crazylifewithlabs Thu 09-Dec-21 11:28:01

Hello lovely fashionista's . I'm in dire need of help! I need a smart casual wardrobe!

3 years ago my husband left for another woman and spent a year making myself feel all fabulous. Then lock down hit.

I have been working from home for 2 years full time as a single parent and trying to just survive and I feel yuck as Ive lived in my old gym clothes and gained weight.

I have a Labrador who covers everything in hairs. So my go to outfit of black dress and black tights and boots isnt an option. Im 5ft 2 and a curvy size 10/12 and totally hate jeans.

Due to an office refurb it will be 6 months before I can get back to the office 2 days (WFH the rest) and the dress policy at work has changed from business dress to smart causal.

So I want to simplify my clothes and have a smart causal wardrobe that I can wear working from home, still be warm and be happy to do the school run with. Does anyone have any suggestions?

Saurus72 Thu 09-Dec-21 14:21:53

I used to really enjoy getting dressed up for work and found the first few months of wfh really hard from that perspective - I missed (still do) that part of my life.

For warmer weather, I bought some shirt dresses from Hush - not my usual shop tbh however I like the fact that I feel reasonably put together while still being comfy. They also dont need ironing. I also have some wrap dresses from Arket which again are comfy but smart-ish. For winter, I bought some cashmere jumpers (Boden, Arket, John Lewis, Jigsaw) and wear those on rotation with a couple of skirts (Toast and Baukjen). I still have different clothes to wear at weekends and evening (Toast and Whistles mainly) so it doesnt feel like Im wearing the same stuff all the time.

I used to have a lovely work wardrobe and also miss dressing up for work.

I've embraced nice loungewear though - can't be bothered to put tights on during the day, so have some really nice leggings or lounge trousers plus cashmere jumpers. (I also have to walk the dogs during the day so this fits in that part of the day).
