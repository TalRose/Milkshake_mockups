Adios, amigo! Yankees release fan favorite from comeback bid

Updated: Jul. 14, 2022, 8:15 a.m. | Published: Jul. 14, 2022, 7:58 a.m.
By Randy Miller | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com

The Yankees used to say Greg Bird’s left-handed swing was made for Yankee Stadium.

They called it pretty.

They thought he’d be Mark Teixeira’s successor at first base and hit a bunch of home runs year after year, and there was some teasing that this would play out. Remember those 11 homers in his first 46 big-league games in 2015? And how about that lefty-on-lefty homer facing Andrew Miller in a 1-0 ALDS win over Cleveland in 2017? Yankee Stadium was rocking that night.

Other than that, Bird was a big disappointment. He couldn’t stay healthy and he didn’t hit much when he was able to play. A bunch of injuries and a couple surgeries led to the Yankees giving up on Bird via November 2019 DFA that led to free agency.

After two seasons away, all of it in Triple-A or alternate camps, Bird and the Yankees reunited this season on a minor-league contract. Some of the old fire was back, he told NJ Advance Media in April while he was a healthy first baseman/DH for the Scranton/Wilkes-Barre RailRiders.

Bird’s body held up so far this season, but staying on the field didn’t lead to offensive numbers warranting considering for a return to the majors.

Far from it.

After batting .218 with six homers, 22 RBI and a .679 OPS in 59 Triple-A games this season, Bird was released on Wednesday. He was let go after starting July 3-for-22 with two RBI in six games.

So now Bird’s career is at a crossroads again. He won’t turn 30 until November, but his only MLB time in the last four years was 10 games with the Yankees in 2019.

There seemed to be realistic hope that Bird’s career was back on track after he raked in Triple-A last season playing in the Colorado Rockies’ organization. In 112 games with the Albuquerque Isotopes, Bird hit .267 with .267 with 27 homers, 91 RBI and .894 OPS. That’s the kind of production the Yankees hoped for, if not more.

Bird rejoined the Yankees on April 4, two days after he released by the Blue Jays.

After the MLB lockout ended, he signed with Toronto on March 13 and then fared well in 10 spring training games, batting .261 with two homers, six RBI and a .958 OPS. The Blue Jays weren’t impressed enough to keep him though, and Bird moved again.

Since leaving the Yankees’ organization the first time, Bird has been a minor leaguer for the Rangers, Phillies, Rockies, Blue Jays and Rockies again. The Rangers called up him up from their alternate site to the big leagues on July 31, 2020, but he strained his calf his first day there, went on the IL before playing a game with Texas and was released on Aug. 11.

During Bird’s first Yankees stint, he was on the injured list for all of the 2016 season, most of 2017, almost half of 2018 and all of 2019 after the first two weeks.
