‘Breaking Bad’ stars Bryan Cranston, Aaron Paul bartend in Stamford to promote Dos Hombres mezcal

Joseph Tucci
May 19, 2022Updated: May 19, 2022 11:49 a.m.
Comments

This is a carousel. Use Next and Previous buttons to navigate

"Blue Sky" isn't the only thing that "Breaking Bad" stars Bryan Cranston (Walter White) and Aaron Paul (Jesse Pinkman) are good at mixing together.

The pair, known for playing drug lords on the hit drama, stopped by Connecticut on Wednesday as part of a tour around the country to promote their mezcal brand, Dos Hombres. The actors came up the east coast to Connecticut after stopping in New Jersey earlier this week, making their way to Stew Leonard's in Norwalk before venturing to Fortina pizzeria in Stamford.

"It's so cool that they're in the area and that they're coming to us," director of operations at Fortina Samantha Melisi told Hearst Connecticut Media earlier in May. "[Dos Hombres] is a brand that we carry, and that's been doing very well. So we're happy for them; we think its great."

During their stop at Fortina, Cranston and Paul acted as guest bartenders, serving their fans to shots of Dos Hombres as well as Fortina's special drink, "Up in Smoke," which includes mezcal, white tequila, lime and chili honey.

Guests weren't the only ones sampling the mezcal. At one point in the event, Cranston was seen chugging a bottle of Dos Hombres, as well as pouring the liquor into the mouths of guests.

Serving up the mezcal was just part of the duo's bartending services — they were also there to mingle with their fans. Paul climbed around Fortina, including on the bar table, to greet and take selfies with his fans. The actor also chowed down on some slices of Fortina's pizza. During both of their stops in Connecticut, they signed bottles of their mezcal for fans.

Fans said that the pair seemed down-to-earth, despite their celebrity status. For Amy Gallo, the biggest surprise was seeing so many fans in attendance.

"I thought it was a great event, and the guys up close and personal seemed like really nice normal people," Gallo said. "It's nice to see celebrities come to Stamford, hopefully [Stamford] will have more events like this in the future."

The "Breaking Bad" actors first launched Dos Hombres in 2019 after looking for a project to work on together. "Dos Hombres" means "two men," and is inspired by their "very special bond," the pair wrote on Instagram when launching the liquor in 2019.

"Knowing that we couldn’t share the screen for quite a while — our thoughts turned to a new project. We sipped cocktails and thought about what it should be," they wrote in a message on the Dos Hombres Instagram account. "The younger one looked at his drink and said, 'You know what we should do? We should do a really special mezcal.'"

The mezcal is produced using Espadin agave from the village of San Luis del Rio, Oaxaca, using a process that the villagers have worked on over generations, the brand's website states.

The brand has won multiple awards including from the USA Spirits Rating, New York World Wine & Spirits Competiton, New York International Spirits Competition, New York Blanco Mezcal of the Year, San Francisco World Spirits Competition, London Spirits Competition, Berlin International Spirits Competition and Berlin Mezcal of the Year.
