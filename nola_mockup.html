    The Saints are built to win now, and these 5 players have to produce to make that happen

    • BY PATRICK MAGEE and LUKE JOHNSON Staff writers
    • Published Jul 7, 2022 at 3:30 am | Updated Jul 8, 2022 at 3:26 pm
    • Published Jul 7, 2022 at 3:30 am | Updated Jul 8, 2022 at 3:26 pm

    The New Orleans Saints fell painfully short of the playoffs in 2021, watching the San Francisco 49ers clinch the final spot in the NFC field late on the final day of the regular season.

    During a season in which little went right for the Saints, they fell just a hair shy of the postseason at 9-8.

    Hiring Dennis Allen to replace Sean Payton as head coach was an attempt at continuity for a franchise that believes it is built to win now, and it will be up to the group that general manager Mickey Loomis has gathered to deliver.

    Some positions come with more pressure than others, and these five players have to produce up to expectations this season if the Saints are to reach the playoffs:

    Trevor Penning, left tackle

    If you pick a player in the first round of the NFL draft, the expectation is that he will get on the field sooner rather than later.

    Penning may not start against the Atlanta Falcons in the season opener, but the Saints need the 19th overall selection to catch on quickly to provide depth on the offensive line. At 6-foot-7, 325 pounds, he has all the physical attributes youd want out of an offensive tackle, and a healthy mean streak on top of that

    As a product of Northern Iowa, hell be forced to make a big leap in the quality of competition.

    James Hurst, a nine-season veteran, is ready to roll at left tackle if the rookie needs time to catch on, but a fast-emerging Penning would be a big boost for the offense.

    Marcus Davenport, defensive end

    When hes playing, hes pretty good.

    Unfortunately, the Texas-San Antonio product has battled injuries throughout his first four years in the NFL. During that stretch, he has played in 48 of the Saints 65 regular-season games.

    In the offseason, he had the top portion of his left pinkie finger amputated after dealing with an infection. He had undergone multiple surgeries on the finger, which was first injured during his junior year at UTSA.

    Davenport played some of his best football in 2021, registering a career-high nine sacks in 11 games.

    If he can stay on the field and unleash his potential opposite All-Pro Cam Jordan, the Saints should top their 37 sacks from a year ago.

    Tyrann Mathieu, strong safety

    With Malcolm Jenkins entering retirement, Mathieu seems an ideal fit for what the Saints need at safety.

    While Jenkins was as steady as they come, he wasnt much of a ball hawk with 10 interceptions over seven seasons with the franchise. The 30-year-old Mathieu has 26 career interceptions, including 13 the past three seasons with the Kansas City Chiefs.

    In a league where 30-year-old defensive backs often arent considered top free agent targets, the Saints made an investment in Mathieu that needs to pay off quickly both in terms of production and leadership.

    Jameis Winston, quarterback

    In seven games as the starting quarterback in New Orleans, Winston looked like he put his turnover-plagued play in the past, firing 14 touchdowns against only three interceptions.

    The Saints were 5-2 during that stretch as Winston and Sean Payton worked well together to run a fairly conservative offense.

    He appears on pace to be ready for the season opener after a knee injury that ended his 2021 season early.

    With Payton no longer on the sideline, itll be interesting to see how Winston and offensive coordinator Pete Carmichael put together this years scheme. It would make sense that the Florida State product be given more freedom with a much deeper receiving corps.

    The pressure is on Winston to show he can put up bigger numbers, especially with the uncertainty surrounding Alvin Kamara and his legal battle.

    If the Saints are going to compete for the NFC South title, Winston has to play near a Pro Bowl level.

    Michael Thomas, wide receiver

    After missing 26 games the past two seasons because of injury, Thomas needs to get on the field in 2022.

    Thomas, who signed a five-year, $96 million contract before the start of the 2019 season, was one of the best players in football the last time he was fully healthy. The Saints would greatly benefit from something even close to his 2019 season, when he was named The Associated Press Offensive Player of the Year.

    Thomas missed minicamp, but Allen is upbeat about the receivers progress as he prepares for the season.

    With Jarvis Landry and first-round pick Chris Olave added to the roster, the Saints will have more quality options at receiver than theyve had in quite some time.

    While it seems unlikely Thomas will catch 149 passes in a season like he did in 2019, the 29-year-old needs to return to his No. 1 status in the passing attack.

    There is no shortage of New Orleans Saints players looking to make a major comeback after a trying 2021 season.
